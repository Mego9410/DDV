The reality

What ownership actually feels like

This is what running a dental practice actually looks like. Not the career you imagined.

01

Body tapping out

The physical toll mounts and only gets harder with age. Owners want out before a health scare or worn-down body forces the decision — selling from a tired place, not a chosen one.

02

Team headaches

Key associates leaving can hit your gross just when you’re about to sell. Nurses, receptionists and TCOs drain time and energy. One key person leaving suddenly rocks the whole practice.

03

Admin pile-up

The CQC wants a report. The GDC wants evidence. The NHS wants your UDA numbers. Your HR software wants a login you can’t remember. Meanwhile, your patients are waiting.

The deal table

The biggest financial transaction of your life. One shot at it.

The offer sounds good. But what does “good” actually mean? The headline figure can be real and the structure still hands half back — deferred payments, earn-outs tied to targets you can’t control, a tie-in keeping you working for someone else for years.

The most dangerous deals come wrapped in relationships. A corporate. An associate. Someone you trust. The goodwill disarms you and you stop benchmarking. Most sellers only discover what a good deal looked like after they’ve accepted a worse one.

01

Why benchmark

You need a reference point

Most owners know their own figures — but not whether those figures are typical, high or low for a practice like theirs. That gap matters long before any offer lands.

  1. 01

    Flying blind on the figures

    You know your turnover and costs, but not how they compare to similar practices in your area. Without that context, every conversation — with a buyer, a bank or yourself — starts from guesswork.

  2. 02

    The mix keeps shifting

    A key associate leaves, NHS mix changes, rent creeps up — your figures move and it’s hard to tell what’s normal drift and what’s a real outlier. A benchmark shows you what’s changed against your peers.

  3. 03

    Admin eats the analysis

    CQC, GDC, NHS, HR — the admin never stops, and comparing your numbers to the market never reaches the top of the list. The report does that comparison for you in minutes.

The reason it matters

One shot at the biggest transaction of your life

The offer sounds good. But what does “good” actually mean? The headline figure can be real and the structure still hands half back — deferred payments, earn-outs tied to targets you can’t control, a tie-in keeping you working for someone else for years.

The most dangerous deals come wrapped in relationships. A corporate. An associate. Someone you trust. The goodwill disarms you and you stop benchmarking — and most sellers only discover what a good deal looked like after they’ve accepted a worse one.

You can’t benchmark what you haven’t tested. The Vault is the test.
